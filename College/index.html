Welcome!

Context

This project had a theme of the new dawn, so my plan was to create a post-apocalyptic game environment. The purpose of this is to create an environment users can walk around in. The aim for the environment is to create a map; the purpose is to create an area that has decrepit buildings that clash with the opposite of flourishing life for an open-world environment.

To achieve a good product that is of high standard and usable in the industry, I will use 3ds Max and Substance Painter to create high-poly models and high-resolution textures. The concepts I have thought about include scenery of plants growing through cracks and ivy along building walls. The theme will be further built upon by having a sunrise to subconsciously match the theme.

A key feature to make this project possible is height and normal maps, which will allow for details such as cracks to be possible, which otherwise may not have been possible. As well as this, I am going to use masks in texturing a lot to apply details to select locations. The industry requires models that have no rat's nests or other problems such as overlapping faces, and I will stay vigilant to prevent problems like this.

Finally I will import the models and textures to unity and add a player pod, 3 way lighting and sound effects as well as using the player camera follow presets.

Research

During this project, I had to complete a large amount of research to push my skills. First of all, I needed to learn how to create plants. To do this, I learned from several tutorials. Some of these tutorials have been linked below. Beyond this, I researched what makes an apocalyptic scene successful and found out about how small things such as sound and background create immersion which, if broken, can ruin the experience. The topologies of the plants were hard to make and required looking into how real plants looked and other people's models and their wireframes to understand their design. 

Looking into other post-apocalyptic environments helped me develop the idea of plant life beginning to come back to life despite the environment. Games such as Fallout 4 explored this sort of idea. 

Practical Skills

In this project I had to use a wide range of practical skills one such skill used early on was the use of modifiers without witch this project would not be possible the building relied on the use of the symmetry modifier which allowed me to build one side of the building and it to be automatically replicated on the other side of the axis. Other Modifiers in 3ds max such as the hair and fur modifier where highly important for the creation of plants.

Another Skill I used was sculpting allowing me to shape a plane into a hilly terrain this was done within 3ds max although if I used specific sculpting software I could have done this to a higher standard.

Moving on to the texturing, UV unwrapping was key and was the main skill I used as well as baking my models and using substance painters vast array of paint brushes and I also took advantage of normal and height maps to add details and decals.

Evaluation and reflection

Reflecting on the project I could have made a larger scene and been more ambitious with creative ideas, also some of the texturing could have been made to a higher resolution, the models are good but still could have been more complex in order to push myself.


This scene was my first to have landscape and plants and I'm happy with how the 3d  models and textures  turned out. The project allows for a player to walk round and if i was going to do it again I would add plant physics when you walk trough them however I'm happy with the sounds as you walk through the area.


In conclusion the project is a success as I learned many new things but would improve if done again.
